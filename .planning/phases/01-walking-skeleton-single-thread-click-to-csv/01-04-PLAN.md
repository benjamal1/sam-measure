<objective>
Deliver the segmentation ENGINE — the non-GUI core that can be built and exercised tonight on the
CPU-only build box against real thread photos: `sam2_session.py` (device fallback `cuda>mps>cpu` +
`PYTORCH_ENABLE_MPS_FALLBACK` + forced float32, load-once predictor, `predict_mask()` — SEG-01, SEG-03,

— EXPT-01/02/03). The interactive matplotlib click loop that a human drives is Plan 05; here every
capability is reachable by supplying click coordinates programmatically, so it is real and testable
without a display.

fallback the Mac uses for MPS) and the highest data-integrity requirement (EXPT-02 never touching the
irreplaceable Nextcloud source). Separating the engine (testable now) from the GUI (validated on the Mac
tomorrow) is the environment-mandated split: strong automated coverage of the predictor/export math,
plus a deferred human check of the click UX.

Output: three `src/segment/` modules + tests, including a `slow`-marked smoke test that runs real SAM2
inference on a real photo with a hardcoded point and asserts a non-empty mask + an exported,
source-untouched mask/overlay pair (AC-001/002/003/005 automated portions).
